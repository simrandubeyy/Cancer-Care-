You Beat Cancer By How You Live, Why You Live And In The Manner In Which You Live

  • Health Care
  • 05 Comments

There’s nothing like sitting in a chair after surgery, feeling horrible – in pain and, to be honest, feeling sorry for yourself, and then having your child come into the room and just completely melt down. The mom guilt over not being able to get up and help them is something else!

Many girls picture their wedding all the way down to the shoes. I was never that girl. Yes, I wanted to marry the man of my dreams, but I never envisioned my wedding. "CHALLENGES YOUNG ADULTS SHOULD NEVER HAVE To FACE:"
My husband, Joseph (JJ), and I met while working at a popular retail store when we were 21. (He was that shirtless dude in the front.) We started dating, and our love grew stronger each day. Four years later, in 2004, I was diagnosed with breast cancer. JJ and I were faced with a challenge young adults should never have to face. We still weren't engaged, but JJ said to me, "I am never leaving your side. "Since my breast cancer diagnosis, our journey with cancer has been a long one. I was subsequently diagnosed with two more cancers and had three recurrences. As we learned, I have a rare genetic disorder called Li-Fraumeni syndrome. My body is lacking a P53 gene, which helps prevent cancer. Cancer will be a permanent fixture in my life In 2011, we got engaged on April Fools' Day. I had no idea JJ was going to pop the question. He said, "Even though I married you the day you were diagnosed, we really just need to make it official." And I definitely didn't imagine I'd be planning a wedding while undergoing chemo.

“People ask if I had it to do again, would I choose cancer? Yes.”beacuse it has brought my love of life in my life.

“I won’t let cancer define me, but I’ll use my journey to help others.”
